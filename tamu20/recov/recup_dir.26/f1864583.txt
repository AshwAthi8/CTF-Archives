<A NAME="#offlinemail"></a>
<p class="title">How to use offline <nobr>e-mail</nobr></p>

<p><b>If you are <span id=oISPNameAnOrA>an MSN Internet Access</span>&nbsp;member, you can use MSN Explorer to read and write
 <nobr>e-mail</nobr> when you are not connected to the Internet.</b></p>
 When you choose to prepare your mail for offline use, MSN Explorer downloads your <nobr>e-mail</nobr> 
to your local computer. When you are working offline, you can read and respond to e-mail
  the same way you work online. MSN Explorer knows that you want
   to remain offline until you choose to go online. The next time you go online,
   <nobr>e-mail</nobr> in your Outbox is sent, <nobr>e-mail</nobr> marked for deletion is removed, and 
   all other actions taken offline are completed. </p>

<p class="header2">To use offline <nobr>e-mail</nobr> you need to do these three steps:</p>

<ol><li>While online, prepare your <nobr>e-mail</nobr> for offline use.</li><br>
	<ul><li>In MSN Explorer E-mail, click <b>My <nobr>E-mail</b></nobr>.</li><br>
	<li>Above the list of messages in your Inbox, click <b>More</b> and 	
	choose <b>Prepare Mail for Offline</b>.</li></ul><br>
<li>Sign out of MSN Explorer.</li><br>
<ul><li> On the sign-in screen, click <b>Disconnect</b> to go offline. </li></ul><br>
<li>Sign back in to MSN Explorer as an offline user.</li><br>
<ul><li>On the sign-in screen, click your <nobr>e-mail</nobr> (sign-in) name.</li><br>
<li>Underneath your password, select the check box<b> Read mail offline</b>,
and then click <b>Sign In</b>.</li></ul></ol><br>

<A NAME="passwords"></a>
<p class="title">How to create a good password</p>
<p class="header2">What is a password? </p>
<p>A password is a combination of characters and numbers that is used in conjunction 
with your account name to sign in to your account.
Like a key, your password restricts access to your account.</p>

<p>Your password will always show up on the screen as asterisks
so no one can see it and sign in without your permission. 
Memorize your password and be cautious about sharing your password with others. </p>

<p class="header2">Password Rules</p>
<p>The foundation of online safety is a strong, secret password. 
Use the following password rules and tips
when creating or changing your password.</p>
<ul>
  <li>
    Your password must be at least 8 characters long, but no longer than 16 characters.
    It may contain numbers (0-9) and upper and lowercase letters (A-Z, a-z), but no blank spaces.
</li>
  <li>
   Your password is case-sensitive, so note which letters you capitalize.</li>
  <li>
   Use a combination of letters and numbers, such as px3up469.</li>
</ul>
<BR>
<p class="header2">Password Tips</p>
<p>Never use a password that is
easy to guess. Your birth date, Social Security number, and children's names are
easy targets.</p>
<p>Your password should not
contain your e-mail name, your first name or last name.</p>
<p>Never write your password on
paper. Use a password you can remember.</p>
<p>Don't use dictionary words
from any language.</p>
<p>Never send your password by
e-mail or in an instant message.</p>

<A NAME="secure"></a>
<p class="title">Why it is safe to send credit card information over the Internet</p>
<p>You can safely send your credit card information over the Internet because MSN 
uses secure server technology and Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) to securely
 transmit your credit card number over the Internet. </p>

<p>Secure Sockets Layer is the industry standard in Web security. 
It is used primarily to transmit sensitive information over the Internet. 
When you have a secure connection between your browser and a Web site, 
no one else can easily access the data that you send across the connection.
 MSN uses SSL to encrypt your sign-in name, password, and wallet to
  give you a high level of security.</p> 

<p>Occasionally, your browser might display security alerts
 to notify you that you are entering or leaving a secure connection. 
 These alerts tell you when MSN is taking precautions to keep your private information (such as your password) secure while it travels from
  your computer to the MSN server. This is normal, and you can choose to continue 
  when these alerts appear.</p>

<A NAME="wallet"></a><p class="title">About Passport wallet</p>
				
				
<p>Your Passport wallet is much like the wallet you carry around in your back pocket or your purse.
When you enter a retail shop and you see something you want to buy, you
pull out your wallet and choose the appropriate credit card to hand to the merchant.
After the merchant authorizes your card, you get the merchandise, and you're on your way.</p>

<p>Your Passport wallet works in a similar way when you shop online, and you can use it at
a growing number of online merchants. In your Passport wallet, you can store as many credit
cards and shipping addresses as you want. When you make an online
purchase, you choose which card and shipping address you want to use, and you send only
that information to the online merchant over a secure connection. The online merchant then
uses the information to authorize and deliver your purchase.</p>

<p>By storing your credit card and address information in your Passport wallet, you don't need to
retype it every time you make an online purchase. This will make your online purchases 
safer and faster.
</p>
<p><b>Note:</b> Your wallet accepts all major credit and charge cards, as well as debit 
cards that do not require a PIN (personal identification number) to use.</p>
<p>To learn more about Passport wallet, go to www.passport.com.</p><br>
